Case Study

WebOps IITM
Website

Designed and built the digital home for IIT Madras's Web Operations club from scratch.

PREVIEW

ROLE

Designer & Developer

TIMELINE

3-4 Weeks (2023)

TOOLS

HTML/CSS/JS, WebGL

The Challenge

As a Super Coordinator of the WebOps club at IIT Madras, I noticed a critical irony: our club, focused on web development, had no digital presence. While we built sites for others, we lacked our own platform.

I needed to build a site that would recruit members, showcase our portfolio, and prove our technical expertise—all while balancing academic responsibilities.

Key Objectives

  • Establish a professional digital identity.
  • Create a recruitment funnel for students.
  • Showcase past projects to campus organizations.
  • Ensure performance on slow campus networks.

Visual Strategy

The goal was to strike a balance: professional enough to build credibility, but approachable enough to feel like a student community. I opted for a Dark Mode aesthetic which is popular in developer communities.

Color Palette

I chose a deep dark background to reduce eye strain and a vibrant purple accent to signal creativity and innovation.

#121212
#4D16BE
#FFFFFF

Typography

Headings: Josefin Sans (Geometric, Impactful)

Body: Poppins (Modern, Readable)

The combination creates a tech-forward look that establishes hierarchy clearly.

Key Design Decisions

01. Glassmorphism Navigation

I designed a fixed navigation bar with a pill-shape and glassmorphism effect. It stays accessible while scrolling but visually "floats" above the content, maintaining the modern aesthetic without cluttering the view.

02. Interactive Project Showcase

To prevent information overload, I implemented a horizontal scroll for project cards. Hovering over a card expands a colored "bulb" background, providing instant visual feedback and encouraging interaction.

03. 3D Team Cards

Team sections are often boring. I utilized CSS 3D transforms to create cards that flip on hover. This "gamifies" the discovery of information (revealing roll numbers and roles) and showcases our CSS capabilities.

04. WebGL Fluid Simulation

For the Application page, I integrated a WebGL fluid simulation. It serves as a "wow factor" that differentiates us from other clubs and demonstrates technical prowess to prospective recruits.

Technical Implementation

The Stack

I deliberately avoided heavy frameworks to ensure raw performance and to demonstrate fundamental understanding.

HTML5 CSS3 Vanilla JS WebGL

Performance

Optimized for campus networks via lazy loading, compressed assets, and texture downsampling for the WebGL elements on mobile devices.
